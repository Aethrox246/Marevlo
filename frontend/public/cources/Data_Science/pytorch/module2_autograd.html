1.ComputationGraphIntuition

WhatisaComputationGraph?

EverytimeyoudomathwithPyTorchtensorsthathaverequires_grad=True,PyTorch silentlybuildsadirectedacyclicgraph(DAG)inthebackground.Eachnodeinthisgraph iseither:

•Atensor(thedata),or
•Afunction(anoperationlike+,*,sin,matmul)

Whenyoucall.backward(),PyTorchtraversesthisgraphinreverse—fromtheoutputback totheinputs—applyingthechainruleofcalculusateachsteptocomputegradients.

Real-WorldAnalogy:AFactoryAssemblyLine(inReverse)

Imagineafactory:rawmaterialsgoin,aproductcomesout.Tofigureouthowmucheachraw materialcontributedtothefinalproduct’squality,youtracebackwardsthroughtheassembly steps—that’sexactlywhatbackpropdoes.

x[square]x² [addb]x²+b [multiplyw]w(x²+b)=loss

TheClassicGradientAccumulationBug
This is one of the mostcommonPyTorchbugs — gradients accumulate across .backward() callsunlessyouexplicitlyzerothem.

x=torch.tensor(2.0,requires_grad=True) #SimulatingatrainingloopWITHOUTzeroinggradients forstepinrange(3): loss=x**2 loss.backward() print(f"Step{step}:x.grad={x.grad.item()}") #4,8,12—keepsadding! #Thecorrectpattern:zerogradientsbeforeeachbackwardpass x.grad.zero_() #oruseoptimizer.zero_grad()intraining forstepinrange(3): ifx.gradisnotNone: x.grad.zero_() #←CRITICAL:clearbeforeeachstep loss=x**2 loss.backward() print(f"Step{step}:x.grad={x.grad.item()}") #4,4,4—correct!

4.NumericalGradientChecking
How do you know your custom backward is correct?Numericalgradientchecking computes thegradientbyfinitedifferencesandcomparesittoyouranalyticalgradient.

Thefinitedifferenceformula:df/dx(f(x+)-f(x-))/(2)

<python>

defnumerical_gradient(f,x,eps=1e-5): grad=torch.zeros_like(x) x_flat=x.view(-1) grad_flat=grad.view(-1) foriinrange(len(x_flat)): x_plus =x.clone();x_plus.view(-1)[i] +=eps x_minus=x.clone();x_minus.view(-1)[i]-=eps grad_flat[i]=(f(x_plus)-f(x_minus))/(2*eps)

"""Computegradientofscalarfunctionfattensorxusingfinitedifferences."""

returngrad#Testfunction:f(x)=sum(x^3) deff(x): return(x**3).sum()x=torch.tensor([1.0,2.0,3.0],requires_grad=True)#Analyticalgradient y=f(x) y.backward() analytical=x.grad.clone()#Numericalgradient numerical=numerical_gradient(f,x.detach()) print("Analyticalgradient:",analytical) print("Numerical gradient:",numerical) print("Maxdifference: ",(analytical-numerical).abs().max().item()) #Shouldbeverysmall(<1e-5)—confirmsourbackwardiscorrect Analyticalgradient:tensor([3.,12.,27.]) Numerical gradient:tensor([3.0518,12.2070,27.0844]) Maxdifference: 0.20703125
