1.ComputationGraphIntuition

WhatisaComputationGraph?

EverytimeyoudomathwithPyTorchtensorsthathaverequires_grad=True,PyTorch silentlybuildsadirectedacyclicgraph(DAG)inthebackground.Eachnodeinthisgraph iseither:

•Atensor(thedata),or
•Afunction(anoperationlike+,*,sin,matmul)

Whenyoucall.backward(),PyTorchtraversesthisgraphinreverse—fromtheoutputback totheinputs—applyingthechainruleofcalculusateachsteptocomputegradients.

Real-WorldAnalogy:AFactoryAssemblyLine(inReverse)

Imagineafactory:rawmaterialsgoin,aproductcomesout.Tofigureouthowmucheachraw materialcontributedtothefinalproduct’squality,youtracebackwardsthroughtheassembly steps—that’sexactlywhatbackpropdoes.

TheClassicGradientAccumulationBug
This is one of the mostcommonPyTorchbugs — gradients accumulate across .backward() callsunlessyouexplicitlyzerothem.

x=torch.tensor(2.0,requires_grad=True)
#SimulatingatrainingloopWITHOUTzeroinggradients forstepinrange(3):
loss=x**2
loss.backward()

print(f"Step{step}:x.grad={x.grad.item()}")

#4,8,12—keepsadding!

#Thecorrectpattern:zerogradientsbeforeeachbackwardpass

x.grad.zero_()

#oruseoptimizer.zero_grad()intraining

forstepinrange(3):
ifx.gradisnotNone:

x.grad.zero_()

#←CRITICAL:clearbeforeeachstep

loss.backward()

print(f"Step{step}:x.grad={x.grad.item()}")

classClampedReLU(torch.autograd.Function): """
AReLUthatclampsoutputto[0,cap].

Forward:

f(x)=clamp(x,0,cap)

Backward:gradient=1if0<x<cap,else0 """

@staticmethod
defforward(ctx,x,cap=1.0):

ctx.save_for_backward(x)

#Saveinputforuseinbackward

ctx.cap=cap
returnx.clamp(0,cap)

@staticmethod
defbackward(ctx,grad_output):
x,=ctx.saved_tensors
cap=ctx.cap
#Gradientpassesthroughonlywhere0<x<cap
grad_input=grad_output*((x>0)&(x<cap)).float()

returngrad_input,None

#Noneforcap(notatensor)

#Testit

print("Forward:",y)

#[0,0.5,1.0,1.0]

loss=y.sum()
loss.backward()

print("Gradients:",x.grad)

#[0,1,0,0]—onlymiddlevaluepassesgrad
